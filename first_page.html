Fundamental Rights

India, also known as Bharat, is a Union of States. It is a Sovereign Socialist Secular Democratic Republic with a parliamentary system of government. The Republic is governed in terms of the Constitution of India which was adopted by the Constituent Assembly on 26th November, 1949 and came into force on 26th January, 1950.

Fundamental Rights are the basic rights that every person in India is guaranteed by the Constitution. These rights are meant to protect you and make sure you are treated fairly by the government and others.

Simple Features of Fundamental Rights:

1) For Everyone: These rights apply to all Indian citizens equally, no matter who you are or where you're from.

2) Can Go to Court: If someone takes away your rights, you can go to court to protect them.

3) Part of the Constitution: These rights are written in the Indian Constitution, which means they are very important and cannot be taken away easily.

4) Balanced with Responsibilities: While you have these rights, you also have duties towards the country and others.

Types of Fundamental Rights:

1) Right to Equality

2) Right to Freedom

3) Right against Exploitation

4) Right to Freedom of Religion

5) Cultural and Educational Rights

6) Right to Constitutional Remedies
